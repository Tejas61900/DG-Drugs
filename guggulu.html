Guggulu (Commiphora mukul)

The Sacred Resin with Healing Properties

Introduction

Guggulu, also known as Commiphora mukul, is a sacred resin used extensively in Ayurvedic medicine. It is valued for its numerous therapeutic properties. Guggulu belongs to the Burseraceae family and has been classified under various traditional medicinal classifications.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Indian Bdellium

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Guggul, Mukul
  • Gujarati: Gugad
  • Hindi: Googal, Guggul
  • Kannada: Guggul
  • Tamil: Meshakshi, Gukkal
  • Telugu: Guggilam

Sanskrit Synonyms:

Kaushika: Associated with its qualities.

Palamkahsha: Descriptive of its healing abilities.

Pura: Refers to its historical use.

Kumbholukhala: Another synonym highlighting its medicinal use.

Mahishaksha: Indicative of its potent nature.

Devadhupa: Used in sacred rituals.

Morphology

Commiphora mukul is a small to medium tree known for its oleo-resin (gum). It is characterized by its unique gum resin which is harvested and used for various therapeutic purposes.

Distribution

Guggulu is found predominantly in the Indian subcontinent.

Uses

Guggulu is known for its various medicinal uses, including:

  • Anartava: Used with kasis and aloe to treat amenorrhea in adult girls.
  • Aamavata: Combined with cow urine for rheumatic arthritis.
  • Arsha: Applied as lepa and fumigation for hemorrhoids.

Dosage

The typical dosage of Guggulu resin is between 250 mg to 1 gram.

Formulations

Common formulations include:

  • Kaishor Guggulu
  • Kanchanar Guggulu
  • Yogaraja Guggulu
  • Sinhnad Guggulu
  • Trayodashanga Guggulu

Adverse Effects

Adverse effects are not commonly known, but always use under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
