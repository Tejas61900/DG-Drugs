Jambu (Syzygium cumini)

The Jamun Tree with Significant Therapeutic Benefits

Introduction

Jambu, commonly known as Jamun, is a medium-sized tree with numerous medicinal properties. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Jamun

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Kala Jam
  • Gujarati: Jambu
  • Hindi: Jamun
  • Tamil: Saval Naval
  • Telugu: Naredu Chettu

Sanskrit Synonyms:

Phalendra: Associated with its fruit.

Surabhipatra: Known for its aromatic leaves.

Jambava: A traditional name used in various texts.

Kokilesta: Reference to its pleasant qualities.

Pikabhaksha: Related to its therapeutic use.

Morphology

Jambu is a 10-12 feet medium tree, known for its fruit, leaves, and stem bark, which are used in various medicinal preparations.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under mutrasangahaniya, chardinigrahana, purishvirajaniya.

Aacharya Sushruta & Acharya Vagbhata: Included in nyagrodhadi gana.

Uses

Jambu offers numerous therapeutic benefits, including:

  • Raktapitta: Hima preparation of jambu, arjuna, and amra helps in managing bleeding disorders.
  • Atisara: Powder of seed with buttermilk is effective in treating diarrhea.
  • Prameha: Seed powder or tablet mixed with honey aids in managing diabetes.

Dosage

The recommended dosage is:

  • Seed Powder: 3-6 gm
  • Fruit Juice: 10-20 ml

Formulations

Various formulations include:

  • Jambavadi Ghrita
  • Jambavadi Taila
  • Jambavasava
  • Pushyanuga Churna

Adverse Effects

Adverse effects are not known; however, it is advised to use under professional supervision.
