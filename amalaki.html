Amalaki (Amla) Ayurvedic Herb

About Amalaki (Amla)

Amalaki, also known as Amla, is one of the most widely used Ayurvedic ingredients. It is both a food and medicine, packed with numerous health benefits. The word "Amla" refers to sour, which is its predominant taste.

Botanical Name

Emblica officinalis or Phyllanthus emblica belongs to Euphorbeaceae family (Eranda kula)

How to Consume Amalaki (Amla)

If fresh Amla fruit is available, it can be eaten raw, or used to make salads, chutney, pickle, or murabba. If fresh Amla is not available, Amla powder can be consumed.

Amla for Vata Balance

If you have Vata imbalance, such as joint diseases or gas trouble, consume Amla with Sesame oil:

  • 1 teaspoon of dried Amla powder mixed with 1 teaspoon of sesame oil, taken with lukewarm water in the evening.

Amla for Pitta Balance

For Pitta balance, especially for digestion problems or skin issues like acne, take Amla with ghee (clarified butter):

  • 1 teaspoon of dried Amla powder mixed with 1 teaspoon of ghee, followed by hot water, in the afternoon.

Amla for Kapha Balance

For Kapha balance, for diseases like obesity or respiratory issues, consume Amla powder along with honey:

  • 1 teaspoon of dried Amla powder mixed with 1 teaspoon of honey, taken in the morning.

Medicinal Qualities

  • Guna (Qualities): Guru (Heaviness), Sheeta (Coolant)
  • Rasa (Taste): Sour, Sweet, Bitter, Astringent, Pungent (excluding salt), with sour being dominant
  • Vipaka (Taste Conversion After Digestion): Madhura (Sweet)
  • Veerya (Potency): Sheeta (Cold)
  • Effect on Tridosha: Tridoshahara (Balances all three Doshas)

Traditional Benefits

  • Vayasthapana - Best anti-aging herb
  • Chakshushya - Improves vision
  • Sarvadoshaghna - Balances Vata, Pitta, and Kapha
  • Raktapittaghna - Useful in bleeding disorders
  • Pramehaghna - Helpful in diabetes and urinary disorders
  • Vrushya - Aphrodisiac
  • Rasayana - Rejuvenative
  • Kanthya - Good for throat diseases
  • Hrudya - Beneficial for the heart
  • Daahahara - Relieves burning sensation
  • Jvarahara - Useful in fever
  • Amalaki churna is used in preparing Vida Lavana (a type of salt)
