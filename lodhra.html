Lodhra (Symplocos Bark)

Traditional Herb for Health and Healing

Introduction

Lodhra, also known as Symplocos Bark, is an important herb in Ayurvedic medicine. Its botanical name is Symplocos racemosa and it belongs to the Symplocaceae family.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Names: Symplocos Bark

Regional Names:

  • Gujarati: Lodhar
  • Hindi: Lodh
  • Marathi: Lodh
  • Telugu: Lodadhuchettu
  • Kannada: Lodhra

Main Synonyms:

Tilva: Known for its traditional uses.

Tirita: Reflects its various properties.

Shavar: Associated with its external applications.

Morphology

Lodhra is a 5-6 meter high tree, with the useful parts being the stem bark and flowers.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under Sandhaniya, Sonitasthapana, Purishsangrahaniya Mahakashaya, and Kashaya Skandha.

Aacharya Sushruta: Included in Lodhradi and Nyagrodhadi Gana.

Aacharya Vagbhata: Part of Rodhradi and Nyagrodhadi Gana.

Therapeutic Uses

Lodhra is known for its diverse therapeutic applications:

  • Rakta Pradara: Bark powder mixed with sugar is effective in treating metrorrhagia.
  • Atisara: For diarrhea, bark powder with buttermilk is beneficial.
  • Akshi Sotha: Bark powder with licorice root powder applied externally helps in eye inflammation.

Dosage

The recommended dosage for Lodhra is:

  • Bark Powder: 3-5 gm
  • Decoction: 50-100 ml

Formulations

Popular formulations of Lodhra include:

  • Rodhrasava
  • Lodhradi Kashaya
  • Nygrodhadi Churna
  • Nyagrodhadi Kwath

Adverse Effects

Currently, there are no known adverse effects associated with Lodhra.

Remedial Measures

No specific remedial measures are required for Lodhra.
