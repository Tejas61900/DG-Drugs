Karkatashringi (Pistacia integerrima)

Essential Herb for Various Therapeutic Uses

Introduction

Karkatashringi, scientifically known as Pistacia integerrima, is a notable herb in Ayurvedic medicine. Commonly referred to as Cowhage or Karkatashringi, this tree belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is recognized for its therapeutic benefits.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Cowhage

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Kankarashrungi
  • Gujarati: Kakda Songi
  • Hindi: Kakdasingi
  • Punjabi: Kakar, Sumak
  • Tamil: Kakkat Shingi
  • Telugu: Kakrsingi

Main Synonyms:

Ajashringi: Indicates the shape and properties of the herb.

Kuliravishanika: A traditional name reflecting its medicinal use.

Vakra: Refers to its curved structure.

Chakra: Symbolizes its therapeutic benefits.

Shringi: Denotes its shape.

Karkatakhya: Associated with its distinctive appearance.

Morphology

Karkatashringi is a 10-16 meter high tree, with galls being the primary useful part of the plant.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under hikkanigrahana, kasahara, and madhuraskandha.

Aacharya Sushruta: Included in kakolyadi and padmakadi.

Bhavprakash Nighantu: Listed under haritakyadi varga.

Uses

Karkatashringi is known for its various therapeutic benefits:

  • Chardi: Karkatashringi powder combined with musta powder helps in controlling vomiting.
  • Kasa: Karkatashringi powder mixed with kantakari decoction is beneficial for cough relief.
  • Vrushya: A paste of Karkatashringi taken with cow milk, sugar, ghee, and milk acts as an aphrodisiac.

Dosage

The recommended dosage for Karkatashringi is:

  • Powder: 1-3 gm

Formulations

Common formulations of Karkatashringi include:

  • Balachaturbhadra Churna
  • Shringyadi Churna

Adverse Effects

There are no known adverse effects associated with Karkatashringi.
