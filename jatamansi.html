Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi)

The Spikenard with Ancient Healing Properties

Introduction

Jatamansi, also known as Spikenard, is a revered herb used for its extensive medicinal benefits. It belongs to the Valerianaeae family and has been utilized in traditional medicine for enhancing intellectual capabilities and treating skin disorders.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Spikenard

Regional Names:

  • Gujarati: Jatamansi
  • Hindi: Jatamansi
  • Tamil: Jatamansi
  • Telugu: Jatamanis

Sanskrit Synonyms:

Tapasvini: Known for its quality of radiating warmth.

Nalada: Related to its beneficial effects.

Pista: Traditional name used in ancient texts.

Mura: A term reflecting its characteristics.

Jata: Refers to its appearance and texture.

Vilomasha: Name highlighting its unique properties.

Morphology

Jatamansi is a perennial herb with a notable rhizome that is primarily used for medicinal purposes. It is well-known for its distinct aroma and therapeutic qualities.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under tikta skanda, kandughna, sangyasthapan ganas.

Aacharya Sushruta: Included in elanjanadi gana, eladi gana.

Acharaya Vagbhata: Also listed in eladi gana and anjandi gana.

Uses

Jatamansi is valued for its versatile therapeutic uses:

  • Vatrakta: A paste made with yastimadhu, Ashvattha, jatamansi, and durva is applied in gout after bloodletting.
  • Anindra: Powder mixed with buffalo's milk at night is effective in treating sleeplessness.
  • Kustha: A mixture of jatamansi powder with indravaruni, Guduchi, and mudgaparni is used externally for skin disorders.

Dosage

The recommended dosage is:

  • Powder: 1-3 gm

Formulations

Various formulations include:

  • Mansyadi Kwath
  • Changeri Ghrita
  • Khadiradi Gutika
  • Mahapadma Taila
  • Madhuparnyadi Taila
  • Abhayanjan Taila

Adverse Effects

Adverse effects are not known; however, it is advisable to use under professional supervision.
