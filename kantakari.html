Kantakari (Solanum surattense/xanthocarpum)

A Prickly Herb with Powerful Therapeutic Benefits

Introduction

Kantakari, also known as Solanum surattense or Solanum xanthocarpum, is a significant herb in traditional medicine. This prickly herb belongs to the Solanaceae family and is renowned for its diverse therapeutic properties.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Staff Tree

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Kantikari
  • Gujarati: Bethi Boringani
  • Hindi: Laghu Katai
  • Kannada: Nella Gullu
  • Tamil: Kandanakatttari
  • Telugu: Challan Mulaga

Main Synonyms:

Kshudra: Refers to its small size and utility.

Duhsparsha: Reflects its prickly nature.

Vyaghri: Indicates its use in treating certain diseases.

Nidigdhika: Traditional name used in various texts.

Kantalika: A term denoting its therapeutic properties.

Kantakini: Known for its medicinal benefits.

Morphology

Kantakari is a diffuse herb covered with prickles, growing in various regions. Its whole plant and root are used in traditional remedies.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under angamarda parshamana, kanthya, hikkanigrahana, sothahara, kasahara.

Aacharya Sushruta: Included in laghypanchamoola, bruhtyadi, varunadi.

Aacharya Vagbhata: Listed under bruhatyadi, varunadi.

Uses

Kantakari is utilized for a range of therapeutic purposes:

  • Kasa: Decoction combined with pippali powder is effective for cough.
  • Swasa: A mixture of Kantakari, amla, and hingu powder with honey helps in asthma.
  • Sotha: Root decoction mixed with guda is beneficial for inflammatory diseases.

Dosage

The recommended dosage is:

  • Decoction: 40-80 ml
  • Powder: 1-3 gm

Formulations

Common formulations include:

  • Kantakari Ghrita
  • Kantakari Avleha
  • Vyaghri Taila
  • Vyaghri Haritaki

Adverse Effects

Adverse effects are not known; however, it is recommended to use under professional guidance.
