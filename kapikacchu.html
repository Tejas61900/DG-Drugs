Kapikacchu (Mucuna pruriens)

A Vital Herb for Stamina and Vitality

Introduction

Kapikacchu, known scientifically as Mucuna pruriens, is a prominent herb in Ayurvedic medicine. Commonly referred to as Cowhage or Kapikachu, this annual herbaceous twinner belongs to the Fabaceae family.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Cowhage

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Aalkushi
  • Gujarati: Kavach, Kauncha
  • Hindi: Kevanch, Kaunch
  • Kannada: Nasugunni
  • Tamil: Punaik Kali
  • Telugu: Pillo Adugu

Main Synonyms:

Atmagupta: A traditional name reflecting its use in strengthening.

Rushyaprokta: Indicates its use in ancient remedies.

Kandura: Denotes its medicinal properties.

Markati: Associated with its appearance and effects.

Sukashimbi: Refers to its therapeutic benefits.

Guptaphala: A term used in classical texts.

Morphology

Kapikacchu is an annual herbaceous twinner with seeds, roots, and pods utilized in various medicinal formulations.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under balya, purishaviranjaniya mahakashaya, and madhura skandha.

Aacharya Sushruta: Included in vidarigandhadi and vatasamsamana.

Aacharya Vagbhata: Listed under durvadi and vidaryadi gana.

Uses

Kapikacchu offers various therapeutic benefits:

  • Sandhivata: Decoction of seeds aids in relieving pain and inflammation in osteoarthritis.
  • Vajikara: Seeds boiled in milk serve as an effective aphrodisiac.
  • Krimi: Pod hair powder combined with navanita or guda helps in treating worms.

Dosage

The recommended dosage for Kapikacchu is:

  • Seed Powder: 3-5 gm
  • Decoction: 40-80 ml
  • Pod Hair Powder: 125 mg

Formulations

Kapikacchu is commonly used in the following formulations:

  • Vanari Vati
  • Kaunchapaka
  • Mashabaladi Taila

Adverse Effects

Kapikacchu can cause irritation and itching if handled without gloves. Ensure proper handling and use of gloves when dealing with the herb.

Remedial Measure: Symptomatic treatment for irritation is recommended.
