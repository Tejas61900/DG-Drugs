Hingu (Ferula northex)

The Asafoetida with Remarkable Therapeutic Properties

Introduction

Hingu, also known as Asafoetida, is a well-known herb used extensively in traditional medicine. It belongs to the Umbelliferae family and is renowned for its distinctive aroma and medicinal benefits.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Asafoetida

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Hing
  • Gujarati: Hing
  • Hindi: Hing
  • Tamil: Perungayam
  • Telugu: Inguva

Sanskrit Synonyms:

Jatuka: Known for its unique properties.

Bahlika: Reference to its historical use.

Ramatha: Traditional name used in various texts.

Sahastravedhi: Associated with therapeutic qualities.

Ugragandha: Noted for its strong odor.

Agudagandha: Known for its potent fragrance.

Supadhupana: Used for its medicinal effects.

Jatughna: Provides relief from certain conditions.

Morphology

Hingu is a 2-3 feet high herb with a distinctive resin that is the primary useful part of the plant. Its resin is utilized in various medicinal preparations.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under swasahara, sangyasthapana, dipaniya, katu skanda.

Aacharya Sushruta: Included in pippalyadi, ushakadi.

Aacharya Vagbhata: Listed in pippalyadi.

Uses

Hingu has several therapeutic applications, including:

  • Dantashoola: Applying a pinch of hingu powder to the affected tooth provides pain relief.
  • Udarshoola: Combining hingu with nimbuka juice helps alleviate abdominal cramps.
  • Madatyaya: Hingu mixed with black pepper and salt is used to counteract effects of alcoholism.

Dosage

The recommended dosage is:

  • Resin Powder: 125-500 mg

Formulations

Various formulations include:

  • Hingvastaka Churna
  • Hingu-Triguna Taila
  • Hingvadi Churna
  • Rajahpravartini Vati
  • Hingvadi Gutika

Adverse Effects

Adverse effects are not known; however, it is recommended to use under professional guidance.
