Kanchanara (Bauhinia Variegata)

The Golden Orchid with Diverse Benefits

Introduction

Kanchanara, also known as Golden Orchid, is a versatile herb used in traditional medicine for its wide range of therapeutic benefits. It belongs to the Caesalpinioideae family and is well-regarded for its efficacy in treating various health conditions.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Staff Tree

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Kanchana
  • Gujarati: Champakati
  • Hindi: Kachnara
  • Punjabi: Kulaad
  • Tamil: Mandare
  • Telugu: Devakachanamu

Main Synonyms:

Gandari: Refers to its therapeutic properties.

Yugmapatraka: A name denoting its traditional use.

Swalpa Keshari: Indicative of its appearance.

Kovidara: Traditional term used in various texts.

Karburdara: Known for its medicinal characteristics.

Sonapushpa: Another name reflecting its value in medicine.

Morphology

Kanchanara is a deciduous tree that grows to a height of 5-10 feet. It is characterized by its attractive flowers and bark, which are utilized in traditional remedies.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under vamanopaga.

Aacharya Sushruta: Included in urdhvabhagahara.

Bhavprakash Nighantu: Listed under guduchyadi varga.

Uses

Kanchanara is employed in various therapeutic applications:

  • Gandamala: Bark powder mixed with kanji and sunthi powder helps in treating lymphadenitis.
  • Sweta Pradara: Bark decoction is used to treat leucorrhea when taken with rice gruel.
  • Atisara: Decoction combined with jeerak powder aids in managing diarrhea.

Dosage

The recommended dosage is:

  • Powder: 3-6 gm
  • Decoction: 40-80 ml

Formulations

Common formulations include:

  • Kanchanara Guggulu
  • Kanchanaradi Kwath
  • Kanchanargulika
  • Kovidaradi Kashaya

Adverse Effects

Adverse effects are not known; however, it is advisable to use under professional guidance.
