Gokshura (Tribulus terrestris)

The Ancient Herb with Versatile Uses

Introduction

Tribulus, known as Gokshura in Ayurveda, is an ancient herb renowned for its medicinal qualities. It has been used for thousands of years for various health benefits. Its botanical name is Tribulus terrestris. In modern times, Tribulus is utilized for bodybuilding, relieving uro-genital diseases, and as an aphrodisiac. It belongs to the Zygophyllaceae family.

Common Names & Sanskrit Synonyms

English Name: Puncture Vine, Small Caltrops, Tribulus, Cowhage

Hindi Name: Gokhru

Sanskrit Synonyms of Gokshura:

Gokshura: The fruit has spines and spreads along the ground, piercing the feet of cows.

Ikshugandhika: Sweet odor like sugarcane.

Trikantaka: Fruit has 3-4 pairs of spines.

Svadukantaka: Spinous fruit with sweet odor and taste.

Shvadamshtra: Toothed with spines.

Kantaphala: Fruits with spines.

Palankasha, Bhakshataka: Fruits injure flesh with sharp spines.

Morphology

Tribulus terrestris is a prostrate shrub with young parts that are silky hairy. The leaves are opposite, paripinnate, with 3-6 pairs of oblong leaflets. The yellow flowers are solitary and hairy. The fruit is globose, light greenish-yellow, with fine hairy or nearly glabrous, often muriculate and woody cocci, each with two pairs of sharp spines, one pair longer than the other. Each coccus contains many seeds.

Distribution

Tribulus terrestris is found throughout India.

Uses

Tribulus is popular in bodybuilding supplements. However, its effectiveness in improving muscle strength and body composition remains unproven clinically.
