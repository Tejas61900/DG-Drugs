Jyotishmati (Celastrus peniculatus)

The Staff Tree with Remarkable Benefits

Introduction

Jyotishmati, commonly known as Staff Tree, is a potent herb used for its diverse medicinal benefits. It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is renowned for its rejuvenating and intellect-enhancing properties.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Staff Tree

Regional Names:

  • Gujarati: Malkangani
  • Hindi: Malkangani
  • Malayalam: Paluruvam

Main Synonyms:

Tejaswini: Reflecting its radiant qualities.

Katabhi: A name indicating its therapeutic use.

Lata: Refers to its climbing nature.

Panya: Traditional term used in ancient texts.

Pita Taila: Refers to its oil in traditional medicine.

Kakadani: Another synonym based on its properties.

Morphology

Jyotishmati is a climbing shrub, known for its climbing nature and medicinal seeds. Its external morphology is characterized by its climbing growth habit and distinctive seeds.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under shirovirechanopaga.

Aacharya Sushruta: Included in shirovirechanopaga, adhobhagahara, and arkadi gana.

Acharaya Vagbhata: Listed under arkadi gana.

Uses

Jyotishmati has a range of therapeutic uses:

  • Gadgadatva: In stammering, 5-10 drops of seed oil in cow milk are administered.
  • Artavakshaya: Leaves with hibiscus flower and kanji help in amenorrhea.
  • Kustha: Jyotishmati oil with apamarga kshar is beneficial for skin disorders.

Dosage

The recommended dosage is:

  • Seed Powder: 1-2 gm
  • Oil: 5-15 drops

Formulations

Common formulations include:

  • Jyotishmati Taila

Adverse Effects

Adverse effects are not known; however, it is recommended to use under professional supervision.
