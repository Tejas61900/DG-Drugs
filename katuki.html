Katuki (Picrorrhiza kurroa)

Valuable Herb in Ayurvedic Medicine

Introduction

Katuki, scientifically known as Picrorrhiza kurroa, is a significant herb in Ayurvedic medicine. Commonly known as Picrorrhiza, this perennial herb belongs to the Scrophularaceae family and is celebrated for its diverse therapeutic properties.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Picrorrhiza

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Kutki
  • Gujarati: Kadu
  • Hindi: Kutki
  • Tamil: Katukarohini
  • Punjabi: Kaundd

Main Synonyms:

Katurohini: Reflects its traditional use in various remedies.

Pitarohini: Indicates its utility in specific therapeutic contexts.

Kastumbhara: Denotes its role in treating specific conditions.

Matsya Shakala: Another traditional name reflecting its properties.

Sakuladani: Associated with its benefits and uses.

Katvi: Traditional name emphasizing its therapeutic effects.

Chakrangi: Refers to its distinct appearance and uses.

Morphology

Katuki is a perennial herb, with the underground stem and root being the primary useful parts of the plant.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under lekhaniya, stanyasodhana, bhedaniya, and tikta skandha.

Aacharya Sushruta: Included in pippalyadi, patoladi, and mustadi.

Aacharya Vagbhata: Listed under patolyadi and mustadi.

Uses

Katuki is known for its wide range of therapeutic benefits:

  • Kustha: Katuki, Chandana, and Ushira powders are used for skin diseases.
  • Pittaja Hridroga: Katuki combined with Licorice root powder is beneficial for cardiac diseases caused by pitta humor.
  • Krimi: Katuki powder mixed with honey helps in expelling worms.

Dosage

The recommended dosage for Katuki is:

  • General Powder: 0.5-1 gm
  • For Purgation: 3-5 gm

Formulations

Common formulations of Katuki include:

  • Aarogyavardhini Vati
  • Katukadhya Ghrita
  • Tikta Ghrita

Adverse Effects

Currently, there are no known adverse effects associated with Katuki.
