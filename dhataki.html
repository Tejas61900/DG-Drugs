Dhataki (Woodfordia fruticosa)

The Fire Flame Bush of Ayurveda

Basonym & Synonyms

Basonym of Drug: Dhataki

Main Synonym: Tamrapushpi, Dhatupushpi, Vahnijwala, Subhiksha, Madyavasini, Kunjara, Madakara, Ratisparsha

Regional Names

Bengali: Dhai

Gujarati: Dhavadi

Hindi: Dhaya

Tamil: Dhatari Jargi

Telugu: Are Puvvu

English: Fire Flame Bush

Botanical Information

Botanical Name: Woodfordia fruticosa Linn.

Family: Lythraceae

Classification (Gana)

Charak Samhita: Sandhaniya, Mutravirajaniya, Purishsangrahaniya

Sushruta Samhita and Acharya Vagbhata: Priyangvadi and Ambasthadi

External Morphology

A small bushy shrub

Useful Parts

Flower

Important Phytoconstituent

Tannins, Glucoside, Nariagenin, Inositol, Hecogenin, Woodferdins A, B, and C.

Rasa Panchak

Rasa: Kashaya

Guna: Laghu, Ruksha

Virya: Shita

Vipaka: Katu

Action

Kaphapittahara

Therapeutic Indication

Madakari (altered consciousness), Raktapittahara (useful in skin and bleeding disorders), Trishnahara (useful in excessive thirst)

Therapeutic Uses

Prajasthapana: For healthy progeny, Nilotpala and Dhataki are given with honey.

Sweta Pradara: Dhataki powder with honey is useful in leucorrhoea.

Krimi: Dhataki powder with Palsha Bija is beneficial as an anti-helminthic.

Dosage

Powder - 1-5 gm

Formulations

Dhatakyadi Churna, Dhatakyadi Taila, Brahat Gangadhara Churna, Arvindasava, Pusyanuga Churna

Adverse Effect

Not Known
