Khadira (Acacia catechu)

Ayurvedic Herb with Diverse Benefits

Introduction

Khadira, known scientifically as Acacia catechu, is a prominent herb in Ayurvedic medicine. Commonly referred to as Black Catechu, this tree belongs to the Mimosoideae family and is valued for its therapeutic properties.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Black Catechu

Regional Names:

  • Gujarati: Khera, Katho
  • Hindi: Kher, Kattha
  • Malayalam: Khair, Kath
  • Tamil: Karngali
  • Telugu: Chandra

Main Synonyms:

Swetasara: Reflects its traditional use in various remedies.

Kadara: Indicates its utility in specific therapeutic contexts.

Somavalakala: Denotes its role in treating specific conditions.

Sweta Khadira: Another traditional name reflecting its properties.

Morphology

Khadira is a tall tree, reaching heights of 15-20 meters. The heartwood, stem bark, and flowers of the tree are the primary useful parts.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Classified under kusthagna, udardaprashamana mahakashaya, and Kashaya skandha.

Aacharya Sushruta: Included in salsari gana.

Aacharya Vagbhata: Listed under asanadi gana.

Uses

Khadira is known for its wide range of therapeutic benefits:

  • Kustha: Decoction of Khadira is beneficial both internally and externally for skin diseases.
  • Shlipada: A paste of Khadirasara, Salasara, and Bijakasara mixed with honey and cow urine is useful in treating filariasis.
  • Dantashoola: Khadirasara powder is used to treat toothache as manjana.

Dosage

The recommended dosage for Khadira is:

  • Bark Powder: 1-3 gm
  • Decoction: 50-100 ml
  • Heartwood Powder: 0.5-1 gm

Formulations

Common formulations of Khadira include:

  • Khadiradi Vati
  • Khadiradhya Taila
  • Khadiraristha

Adverse Effects

Currently, there are no known adverse effects associated with Khadira.
