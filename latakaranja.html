Latakaranja (Physic Nut)

Traditional Herb for Various Ailments

Introduction

Latakaranja, also known as Physic Nut or Bonduc Nut, is a significant herb in Ayurvedic medicine. Its botanical name is Caesalpinia bonducella or Caesalpinia crista Linn, and it belongs to the Fabaceae family.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Names: Physic Nut, Fever Nut, Bonduc Nut

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Kanta Karanja, Nata
  • Gujarati: Kankacha, Kangasiyo
  • Hindi: Karanjava
  • Kannada: Gajjikekai
  • Tamil: Kaza Shikkai
  • Telugu: Gachakay

Main Synonyms:

Kuberaksha: Reflects its protective properties.

Vitapakaranja: Denotes its various uses.

Kantakikaranaja: Traditional name reflecting its applications.

Morphology

Latakaranja is a large scandent shrub known for its distinct appearance. The useful parts of the plant include the root bark, leaves, and seeds.

Classification

Bhavaprakash Nighantu: Included in Guduchyadi Varga.

Kaiyadeva Nighantu: Part of Aushadhi Varga.

Therapeutic Uses

Latakaranja is renowned for its various therapeutic benefits:

  • Atisara: Seeds kernel with kanji is effective in treating diarrhea.
  • Shoola: Seeds powder mixed with Haritaki, Sauvarchala, and Hingu alleviates pain.
  • Raktarsha: Root powder with cow urine helps in managing bleeding piles.

Dosage

The recommended dosage for Latakaranja is:

  • Seed Powder: 60-200 mg
  • Root Juice: 3 ml
  • Bark Powder: 1-3 gm

Formulations

Popular formulations of Latakaranja include:

  • Himasagara Taila

Adverse Effects

Currently, there are no known adverse effects associated with Latakaranja.

Remedial Measures

No specific remedial measures are required for Latakaranja.
