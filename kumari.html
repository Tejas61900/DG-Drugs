Kumari (Aloe Vera)

Versatile Herb with Healing Properties

Introduction

Kumari, scientifically known as Aloe barbadensis, is commonly referred to as Aloe Vera. This perennial small shrub belongs to the Liliaceae family and is renowned for its numerous therapeutic properties.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Aloe Vera

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Ghrutkumari
  • Gujarati: Kuvarpathu
  • Hindi: Gheekunvar, Gavarpatha
  • Kannada: Lolisara
  • Tamil: Katalai
  • Telugu: Kalabanda

Main Synonyms:

Gruhakanya: Reflecting its traditional household use.

Ghritakumarika: Indicates its role in medicinal formulations.

Kanya: Another term reflecting its usage in various remedies.

Morphology

Kumari is a small, perennial shrub characterized by its fleshy, modified leaves that store water. These leaves are the primary part used in medicinal preparations.

Classification

Bhavprakash Nighantu: Included in guduchyadi varga.

Kaiydeva Nighantu: Listed under aushadhi varga.

Raj Nighantu: Part of parpatadi varga.

Uses

Kumari is highly valued for its versatile therapeutic benefits:

  • Kamla: Fresh juice of Kumari is effective in treating jaundice.
  • Vibandh: Fresh juice or pulp taken on an empty stomach aids in relieving constipation.
  • Dagdha Vrana: Aloe vera juice applied topically provides soothing and healing for burns.

Dosage

The recommended dosage for Kumari is:

  • Juice: 10-20 ml
  • Aqueous Extract: 125-250 mg

Formulations

Common formulations of Kumari include:

  • Kumari Ghan
  • Kumari Asava
  • Rajahpravartini Vati
  • Kumari Taila

Adverse Effects

Currently, there are no known adverse effects associated with Kumari.
