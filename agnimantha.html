Agnimantha (Clerodendrum Phlomidis)

Ancient Remedy for Modern Health

Introduction

Agnimantha, also known as Clerodendrum Phlomidis, is a traditional herb widely used in Ayurvedic medicine. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is known for its therapeutic properties.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Names: Physic Nut, Fever Nut

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Ganiyari, Arani, Goniari
  • Gujarati: Arani, Aranimula, Arni
  • Hindi: Urni
  • Kannada: Taggi, Taggi Beru
  • Malayalam: Munja
  • Marathi: Takalimula
  • Oriya: Ganiary
  • Tamil: Tazhutazhai
  • Telugu: Taluki

Main Synonyms:

Ganikarika: Associated with its traditional uses.

Jaya: Reflects its health benefits.

Shriparna: Known for its therapeutic applications.

Morphology

Agnimantha is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 9 meters in height. The useful parts of the plant are the root bark and leaves.

Classification

As per Acharya Charak: Classified under Sothahara, Shitaprashaman, and Anuvasanopaga.

As per Acharya Sushrut: Included in Bruhat Panchamoola, Virtarvaadi, Varunadi, and Vatasamsamana.

Therapeutic Uses

Agnimantha is known for its wide range of therapeutic applications:

  • Odema: Leaves are warmed and tied on the affected spot.
  • Puyameha (Urine Infection): Root decoction is administered twice a day.
  • Urticaria: Root paste is given orally.
  • Post-Fever Weakness: Leaves juice is used to remove weakness.

Dosage

The recommended dosage for Agnimantha is as follows:

  • Powder: 3-6 gm
  • Leaf Juice: 10-20 ml
  • Decoction: 50-100 ml

Formulations

Popular formulations of Agnimantha include:

  • Dashmularishta
  • Dashmula Kwath
  • Agnimanthakashay
  • Dhanvantara Ghrita
  • Gorocanadi Vati
  • Narayana Taila

Adverse Effects

Currently, there are no known adverse effects associated with Agnimantha.

Remedial Measures

No specific remedial measures are required for Agnimantha.
