Kutaja (Kurchi)

Traditional Herb for Diverse Therapeutic Benefits

Introduction

Kutaja, known scientifically as Holarrhena antidysentrica or Wrightia tinctoria, is commonly referred to as Kurchi or Conessi Bark. This tree, belonging to the Apocynaceae family, is highly valued in traditional medicine.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Kurchi, Conessi Bark

Regional Names:

  • Gujarati: Kado
  • Hindi: Koreya, Kuda
  • Malayalam: Pandhra Kuda
  • Tamil: Kodagapala
  • Telugu: Kakakodise

Main Synonyms:

Kalinga: A term reflecting its diverse uses.

Vatsaka: Indicates its historical application.

Indrayava: Another traditional name for the herb.

Morphology

Kutaja is a tall tree, reaching up to 15-20 meters in height. It has various useful parts including seeds, leaves, stem bark, and flowers.

Classification

Aacharya Charaka: Included in kandughna, arshoghna, stanyasodhana, and asthapanopa mahakashaya.

Aacharya Sushruta: Listed under haridradi, lakshadi, aragvadhadi, and pippalyadi gana.

Aacharya Vagbhata: Part of aragvadhadi and pippalyadi gana.

Uses

Kutaja is recognized for its therapeutic benefits:

  • Atisara: Seed and bark powder mixed with buttermilk or honey helps in treating diarrhea.
  • Kandu: Bark powder combined with Khadira churna is applied with Kanji for itching relief.
  • Jwara: Decoction of bark with Katuki churna and rice gruel is effective for fever.

Dosage

The recommended dosage for Kutaja is:

  • Bark/Seed Powder: 3-6 gm
  • Decoction: 50-100 ml

Formulations

Popular formulations of Kutaja include:

  • Kutajaghan Vati
  • Katjadhyavaleha
  • Kutajarista

Adverse Effects

Currently, there are no known adverse effects associated with Kutaja.
