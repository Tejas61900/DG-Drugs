Haridra (Curcuma amada)

The Mango Ginger with Diverse Therapeutic Benefits

Introduction

Haridra, also known as Curcuma amada, is a valuable herb in Ayurveda with a range of medicinal properties. It is commonly referred to as Mango Ginger due to its aroma and flavor.

Common Names & Synonyms

English Name: Mango Ginger

Regional Names:

  • Bengali: Aam Ada
  • Gujarati: Aambbahaldar
  • Hindi: Amiya Haldi, Amahardi
  • Kannada: Huli Aarsin
  • Tamil: Pashu Manjala
  • Telugu: Karupususpu

Sanskrit Synonyms:

Sugandha: Aromatic quality.

Darvibheda: Known for its medicinal properties.

Padmapatra: Reference to its shape.

Aamragandha: Aroma similar to mango.

Darukarpura: Used in traditional medicine.

Surimata: Associated with benefits for the body.

Surataraka: Enhances therapeutic effects.

Morphology

Curcuma amada is a 2-3 feet high shrub with distinctive rhizomes. It has a unique appearance and is characterized by its useful rhizome, which is utilized in various medicinal applications.

Distribution

This herb is commonly found throughout India.

Uses

Haridra is known for its diverse applications, including:

  • Krumi: Rhizome juice mixed with latakaranja leaves is effective against worm infestations.
  • Kandu: Rhizome paste is applied topically for itching relief.
  • Sotha: Hot fomentation of rhizome paste is used to alleviate inflammation.

Dosage

The recommended dosage is:

  • Powder: 3 to 5 grams
  • Juice: 10-20 ml

Formulations

Currently, there are no specific formulations listed for Haridra.

Adverse Effects

Adverse effects are not known, but it's advised to use under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
