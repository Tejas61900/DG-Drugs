Haritaki (Terminalia chebula)

About Haritaki (Terminalia chebula)

Haritaki, also known as Terminalia chebula, is a highly revered rejuvenating herb in Ayurveda. It is part of the Triphala formula and is known for its extensive medicinal properties.

Botanical Name and Family

Botanical Name: Terminalia chebula
Family: Combretaceae (Haritaki kula)

Qualities of Haritaki

  • Rasa (Taste): Astringent (predominant), includes all tastes except salt
  • Guna (Qualities): Laghu (Lightness), Rooksha (Dryness)
  • Vipaka: Madhura (Sweet after digestion)
  • Veerya (Potency): Ushna (Hot)
  • Effect on Tridosha: Balances all three doshas - Vata, Pitta, and Kapha

Medicinal Uses

Haritaki has been traditionally used for a wide range of conditions:

  • Skin diseases (Kushta)
  • Abdominal tumors (Gulma)
  • Abdominal bloating (Udavarta)
  • Inflammation (Shotha)
  • Anemia and liver diseases (Pandu)
  • Delirium (Mada)
  • Hemorrhoids (Arsha)
  • Headaches and diseases related to the head (Shiroroga)
  • Diarrhea and dysentery (Atisara)
  • Coughs and colds (Kasa)
  • Breathing difficulties (Swasa)
  • Diabetes and urinary disorders (Prameha)
  • Bloating and indigestion (Anaha)
  • Jaundice (Kaamala)
  • Eye disorders (Akshiroga)
  • Heart diseases (Hridroga)
  • IBS or sprue syndrome (Grahani)
  • Constipation (Srotovibandha)
  • Memory and intelligence issues (Pramoha, Smritiprada)
  • Skin discoloration (Vaivarnya)
  • Chronic recurrent fever (Purana vishama jwara)
  • And many more...

Haritaki Remedies

  • For Stomatitis and Dental Caries: Gargle with cold infusion of Haritaki fruit
  • For Bleeding Gums: Apply fine fruit powder
  • For Dysentery: Mix 5 grams of stem bark powder with ferula seed soaked water and consume twice a day for two days

Chemical Constituents

Terminalia chebula contains various beneficial compounds including chebulagic acid, chebulic acid, tannic acid, gallic acid, ethyl gallate, ellagic acid, and more.

Morphology and Distribution

Morphology

Terminalia chebula is a medium-sized deciduous tree with oblong-ovate leaves, and ellipsoid to ovoid fruits. Flowers are dull whitish yellow with a strong odor, and seeds are pale yellow.

Distribution

Found throughout South and Southeast Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, Bhutan, Nepal, Bangladesh, Myanmar, Cambodia, Laos, Vietnam, Indonesia, Malaysia, Pakistan, and Thailand.
